Mallalli Falls

Mallalli Falls is a breathtaking waterfall nestled in the Western Ghats of Coorg (Kodagu) district, Karnataka. Formed by the Kumaradhara River, the falls cascade over 200 feet at the foot of the Pushpagiri Hills, creating a mesmerizing two-step waterfall amidst lush greenery.

üåø Highlights

Height & Formation: The falls drop from a height of approximately 200 feet, offering a stunning visual spectacle.
Best Time to Visit: The ideal time is during the monsoon season, from June to September, when the water flow is at its peak, and the surrounding landscape is lush and vibrant.
Activities: Visitors can enjoy a trek to the falls, with concrete steps leading down to the base. The area offers opportunities for photography and nature walks. Swimming is possible but not recommended during peak monsoons due to strong currents.

üïí Visitor Information

By Road: Mallalli Falls is approximately 25 km from Somwarpet, 42 km from Kushalnagar, and 70 km from Madikeri. The nearest village is Kumaralli, about 5 km away. From Kumaralli, a 3 km motorable road leads to Chiguru Homestay, after which a 2 km walk or a jeep ride takes you to the falls.
Public Transport: There are limited bus services from Somwarpet to Kumaralli. Hiring a private vehicle is recommended for convenience.

Precautions: Be cautious of leeches during the rainy season. Avoid crossing barricades and stay on designated paths.

Mandalpatti Peak

Mandalpatti, also known as "Mugilu Pete" (meaning "Market of Clouds"), is a breathtaking viewpoint nestled in the Pushpagiri Reserve Forest near Madikeri, Coorg, Karnataka. Situated at an elevation of approximately 1,600 meters (5,250 feet), it offers panoramic vistas of the Western Ghats, making it a haven for nature lovers, trekkers, and photographers.

üåÑ Highlights of Mandalpatti

Beauty: Experience rolling green hills, dense forests, and mist-covered valleys. The viewpoint provides a 360-degree view of the surrounding landscapes, especially mesmerizing during sunrise and sunset .

üöó How to Reach Mandalpatti

From Madikeri: Mandalpatti is approximately 18 km from Madikeri.
By Jeep: Due to rugged terrain, the last stretch to the viewpoint is best traversed by a 4x4 jeep. Jeeps are available for hire near the KSRTC bus stop in Madikeri .
Trekking: Adventurous travelers can opt for a trek. Vehicles can go up to a certain point, after which a 3 km trek leads to the summit. The trail passes through lush forests and offers glimpses of Coorg's natural beauty .

Tibetan Golden Temple

The Namdroling Monastery, commonly known as the Golden Temple, is a prominent Tibetan Buddhist center located in Bylakuppe, near Kushalnagar in the Kodagu district of Karnataka, India. Established in 1963 by His Holiness Drubwang Padma Norbu Rinpoche, the 11th throneholder of the Palyul lineage, it serves as the largest teaching center of the Nyingma tradition of Tibetan Buddhism in the world

üèØ Key Highlights

Golden Temple (Padmasambhava Buddhist Vihara): Inaugurated in 1999, this temple is renowned for its three towering gold-plated statues:
Buddha Shakyamuni: Standing at 60 feet, representing the historical Buddha.
Guru Padmasambhava: At 58 feet, honoring the founder of Tibetan Buddhism.
Buddha Amitayus: Also 58 feet tall, symbolizing longevity .

üïí Visitor Information

Timings: Open daily from 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM .
Entry Fee: No entry fee.
Photography: Permitted; however, visitors are expected to respect the sanctity of the place.

Etiquette:

Remove footwear before entering temple premises.
Rotate prayer wheels clockwise with the right hand.
Maintain silence and decorum during prayer sessions.

üìç How to Reach

Distance from Kushalnagar: Approximately 6 km .
By Road:
From Mangalore: ~172 km.
From Bengaluru: ~220 km.
Regular bus services are available to Kushalnagar, from where taxis or auto-rickshaws can be hired.

Mandalpatti Peak

Tadiandamol Peak is the highest point in Coorg (Kodagu) district and the third-highest in Karnataka, standing at an elevation of approximately 1,748 meters (5,735 feet) above sea level. Located near the town of Kakkabe, about 35 km from Madikeri, it offers a moderately challenging trek through the scenic landscapes of the Western Ghats.

ü•æ Trek Overview

Trail Length:Approximately 12 km round trip from the base at Nalknad Palace.
Duration: 4 to 6 hours to ascend and descend, depending on pace and weather conditions.
Difficulty: Moderate; suitable for fit beginners and experienced trekkers.

üöó Getting There

By Road: From Madikeri, drive to Kakkabe (approx. 35 km), then proceed to Nalknad Palace, the trek's starting point.
By Public Transport: Buses are available to Kakkabe; from there, hire a local jeep or auto-rickshaw to Nalknad Palace.

üìÖ Best Time to Visit

October to February: Ideal for clear skies and pleasant temperatures.
March to May:Warmer temperatures; carry sufficient water and sun protection.
June to September:Monsoon season brings lush greenery but also slippery trails and leeches; exercise caution

üß≥ Tips for Trekkers

Start Early: Begin your trek by 7:00 AM to avoid afternoon heat and ensure a timely return.
Footwear: Wear sturdy trekking shoes with good grip.
Supplies: Carry sufficient water, snacks, and a basic first-aid kit.
Weather Gear: Bring rain protection during monsoon months.
Permits: Check for any required permissions at the forest checkpoint near the base. Wear sturdy trekking shoes with good grip.

Dubare Elephant Camp

Dubare Elephant Camp is a renowned wildlife sanctuary situated along the banks of the River Cauvery in Kodagu (Coorg), Karnataka. Managed by Jungle Lodges and Resorts Ltd., a unit of the Karnataka government, the camp offers visitors an immersive experience with elephants and the surrounding forest ecosystem.

üêò Elephant Interaction Activities

Feeding Sessions: Participate in feeding elephants with ragi, jaggery, sugarcane, bananas, and coconuts.
Learning Commands: Observe how elephants respond to various commands and learn about their training.
