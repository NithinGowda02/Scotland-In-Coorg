Breathtaking Beauty of Mallalli Falls

Mallalli Falls

Mallalli Falls is a captivating waterfall nestled in the Western Ghats of Coorg, Karnataka. Formed by the Kumaradhara River, the falls cascade over 200 feet at the base of the Pushpagiri Hills, creating a mesmerizing two-tiered drop

Mandalpatti Peak

Mandalpatti is a scenic hilltop viewpoint located approximately 18 km from Madikeri in Coorg, Karnataka. Situated at an elevation of around 1,800 meters, it offers panoramic views of the lush green valleys and rolling hills of the Western Ghats. Often referred to as the "market of clouds" (Muguli-peth), Mandalpatti is renowned for its breathtaking sunrises and sunsets, making it a haven for nature enthusiasts and photographers.

Spiritual Escape: Tibetan Golden Temple

Tibetan Golden Temple

The Namdroling Monastery, popularly known as the Golden Temple, is a significant Tibetan Buddhist monastery located in Bylakuppe, near Kushalnagar in Coorg (Kodagu), Karnataka. Established in 1963 by His Holiness Pema Norbu Rinpoche, it serves as the largest teaching center of the Nyingma lineage of Tibetan Buddhism in the world .

üïíTimings: Open daily from 7:00 AM to 6:00 PM.

Serenity at Abbey Waterfalls, Coorg

Abbey Falls

Abbey Falls, also known as Abbi Falls, is a captivating waterfall located approximately 8 km from Madikeri, the district headquarters of Coorg (Kodagu), Karnataka. Nestled amidst lush coffee and spice plantations, this 70-foot cascade is a popular tourist destination, especially during the monsoon season when the flow is at its peak.

Tranquil Trails to Tadiandamol Peak

Tadiandamol Peak

Tadiandamol, meaning "Tallest Mount" in the Kodava language, stands as the highest peak in Coorg and the third highest in Karnataka, towering at an elevation of 1,748 meters (5,735 feet). Nestled in the Western Ghats, this peak offers trekkers a blend of scenic beauty, rich biodiversity, and cultural heritage.

üïí Best Time to Visit:
